7 Days Before
Stop ibuprofen, vitamins & aspirin.
›

No Advil, Motrin, ibuprofen, or aspirin for seven days before and seven days after surgery. Same goes for all over-the-counter vitamin supplements. The IVAC anesthesia team will likely send a nutrition bundle ahead of surgery — supplement shakes and vitamins to take exactly as prescribed. Those are fine. Everything else stops.

If you're on any prescription medications, IVAC will advise you directly on whether to adjust how you're taking them before the procedure.

Night Before
Wash hair with baby shampoo.
›

Vanderbilt's pre-op team specifically asked for baby shampoo the night before surgery. The incision will be at the back of the skull and down the neck — scalp prep matters. It's also one of those small, specific instructions that makes the night before feel manageable. Something to do instead of just think.

Drink your nutrition shakes tonight if IVAC sent them. Take everything exactly as prescribed.

12:00 Midnight
NPO. Nothing to eat after midnight.
›

NPO stands for nil per os — nothing by mouth. No food, no solid anything after midnight. This is standard before general anesthesia. Having food in your stomach during induction creates a serious aspiration risk. The surgical team will verify this at check-in.

Clear liquids are still allowed until 9 AM — four hours before the 1 PM surgery start. Water, black coffee, apple juice. No pulp, no dairy, nothing with color or substance. When that window closes, it's fully dry NPO until after the procedure.

9:00 AM
Dry NPO. No more clear liquids.
›

The clear liquid window closes at 9 AM — four hours before a 1 PM surgery start. After this, nothing by mouth. Not even water.

This is the moment the waiting actually begins. He's been building to this for months. Now it's just the drive.

11:00 AM
Arrive at Vanderbilt. Main hospital, 2nd floor.
›

Two hours before surgery — exactly where they asked him to be. Main hospital, second floor, surgery check-in. Bring photo ID.

There's construction right now. Free valet is available out front — look for the people in bright red shirts outside the main entrance. They'll take the car. From there it's straight to check-in.

1:00 PM
Surgery begins. 4–6 hours.
›

Surgery is scheduled for 1 PM unless he hears otherwise between now and then. The procedure — suboccipital craniectomy, C1 laminectomy, and duraplasty — runs 4 to 6 hours. The timeline below tracks each stage of the day from here.

Sutures will be placed at the incision and removed at the two-week follow-up appointment. He'll recover at his parents' house in St. Louis — the surgical team can fax suture removal orders to a local provider there.
